So, What Does It All Mean?

Home Temperature Humidity Windiness Cloudiness Data


In conclusion, the latidude of a city only truly impacts that city's temperature. The closer a city is to The Equator (0), the higher its overall temperature is. As we observe the cities that are located further away from the equator, and further into either the Northern or Southern hemispheres, the temperatures start to drop, creating that bell curve on the graph.

In terms of humidity, cloudiness, and windiness, there were other main factors that came into play. For example, when it comes to humidity, a city is more likely to experience highter levels if it is located on/ near a large body/ multiple bodies of water. If the water is not there, the humidity levels are unable to rise beyond a certain point. The role that water plays is much more important than that city's latitude. The same goes for a city's cloudiness and windiness. Since there was very little correlation between latitude and the recorded attributes on all three of these graphs, we must look for other variables that could be affecting them.
