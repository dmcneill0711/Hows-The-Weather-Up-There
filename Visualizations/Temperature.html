Let's Temp These Cities!

Plotting the recorded temperature of a city revealed a very prominent trend in relation to the city's latitude. The closer the city's latitude was to The Equator (0), the higher the recorded temperature. Inversely, the further away we moved from the equator (and further into the Northern and Southern hemispheres), the lower the recorded temperatures became. There were a few outliers (that were located in the Northern hemisphere, which is currently experiencing Spring/Summer right now due to the Earth's tilt), but in the end, this created a bell curve graph that showed that there is a relationship and latitude does play a key role when it comes to determining/ predicting a city's temperature.
